Psych in My Life

This project is a collection of film and TV clips that each demonstrate a psychology term from one of the 13 chapters. Watch each clip and try to choose the correct concept shown in it. Once you make your choice, you can see the correct answer as well as a definition of it and why the clip demonstrates it. Each of the clips has been trimmed for convenience, so some of them may start late and end early.

Chapter 1 - Kingsman: The Secret Service

Context: Eggsy and Roxy are training to become secret agents. For their final test, they are each handed a gun and told to shoot their pet dogs. What they do not know is that their guns are filled with blanks.

Correct Answer: Deception

Definition: Deception occurs when participants in an experiment are misled in order to preserve the double-blindness of the experiment.

Application: The old man is performing an experiment on Eggsy to see if he can kill a dear friend. However, he does not tell Eggsy the nature of this experiment by disguising it as a real test with real consequences (i.e. killing his dog), thus deceiving him.

Chapter 2 - Jurassic Park

Context: Lewis Dodgson is secretly meeting a man called Nedry at an open restaurant while incognito, but Nedry casually uses Dodgson's real name to call him over.

Spotlight Effect

Definition: a phenomenon in which people tend to believe they are being noticed or examined more than they actually are

Application: Dodgson assumes that the people around are paying attention to Nedry when he calls Dodgson over, when in reality, as Nedry says, "Nobody cares."

Chapter 3 - Raiders of the Lost Ark

Context: Indiana Jones encounters an enemy swordsman and is challenged to a duel to the death.

Correct Answer: Overconfidence

Definition: unawareness of one's own errors

Application: The swordsman believes he can easily beat Indiana Jones in a one-on-one duel. However, he is so confident in his own abilities that he forgets you should never bring a sword to a gunfight.

Chapter 4 - Adaptation.
 Language

Context: Charlie Kaufman is a screenwriter desperately struggling with writer's block. As a last-ditch effort, he does something he is vehemently against: Charlie attends a screenwriting class.

Correct Answer: Cognitive Dissonance

Definition: the tension we feel when our attitudes or behaviors don't align with each other

Application: Charlie is going against his beliefs when he attends the class. As a result, he experiences extreme mental tension which we can hear through his inner monologue.

Chapter 5 - Breaking Bad

Context: Walter White has become a meth cook to make money for his family, but now he wants to quit. His boss Gus attempts to convince Walter to keep working for him.

Correct Answer: Gender Roles

Definition: a set of expectations that prescribes how males and females should act, think, and feel in a particular society

Application: Gus illustrates the role of men in society: to provide for their families at all costs, simply, "because he's a man."

Chapter 6 - Shaun of the Dead
 Gore

Context: In a zombie apocalypse, a gang of survivors attempts to walk through a crowd of the undead by acting like fellow zombies.

Correct Answer: Normative Influence

Definition: a tendency to conform in the interest of being perceived favorably by others

Application: In order to avoid getting eaten, the survivors comform to the zombies' behavior, so as not to give themselves away as members of the opposing group: living humans.

Chapter 7 - The Wolf of Wall Street
 Language

Context: Extremely unqualified members of a new brokerage firm have been trained to cold-call clients using a script that helps them achieve sales.

Correct Answer: Authority (persuasion)

Definition: the perception of one's experise, trustiness, and knowledgeability on a given subject

Application: The employees of the firm convince clients to buy stocks by using several techniques to establish themselves as credible sources, such as mentioning their (fake) high-ranking positions and attempt to remain cool and collected over the phone, as if they close large deals everyday.

Chapter 8 - Do the Right Thing
 Language

Context: During a verbal confrontation between the owners of a restaurant and members of the neighborhood, the situation quickly escalates when a man destroys a window.

Correct Answer: Deindividuation

Definition: in large group situations, people are more likely to abandon normal restraints and forget their individual identity

Application: When the man throws a garbage can through the window, several people in the group start looting and destroying the restaurant in full view of the owners. This sort of incident would probably be less likely to occur if the group was a lot smaller or if it was just one person alone. However, in the midst of the chaos, it is easy for everyone to lose their accountability and act out violently.

Chapter 9 - Get Out

Context: A couple has a particularly uncomfortable conversation at a party.

Correct Answer: Stereotypes

Definition: generalizations about a certain group of people

Application: The older woman starts overtly commenting on stereotypes about black men and their bodies. While definitely insensitive, her remarks don't seem to have harmful intentions, so prejudice doesn't quite fit here.

Chapter 10 - Star Wars: The Force Awakens

Context: An unfortunate officer delivers some bad news to his masked commander Kylo Ren.

Correct Answer: Displacement

Definition: an unconscious defense mechanism in which negative emotions are transferred from the original source of the emotion to a less threatening person/object.

Application: Kylo Ren cannot control his anger over the failure of his troops, so he displaces it and takes it out on a nearby console.

Chapter 11 - Mean Girls

Context: Regina (blonde girl) has promised Cady (zombie-costume girl) that she'll talk to Cady's crush Aaron (football player guy) for her. Instead, Regina spreads rumors about Cady and seduces Aaron.

Correct Answer: Physical Attractiveness Stereotype

Definition: the belief that what is attractive is inherently good, and what is unattractive is inherently bad

Application: Aaron is dubious at first about Regina's rumors about Cady despite her attractiveness, but once he sees Cady, who doesn't look particularly attractive in her costume, he starts to believe that Cady is creepy. He also reciprocates Regina's kiss, as he believes she is honest with him. At least part of this is probably due to Regina's physical attractiveness.

Chapter 12 - The Naked Gun: From the Files of Police Squad!

Context: Police lieutenant Frank Drebin bribes a dock worker for information.

Correct Answer: Reciprocity Norm

Definition: the expectation that those we help should help us back, and vice versa

Application: Drebin gives the dock worker money on the assumption that in return, the dock worker will provide information to him. Afterwards, the dock worker does the same thing. Of course, this is an absurd situation, but the moral code is still there!

Chapter 13 - In Bruges
 Language

Context: Ray is hiding in a hotel from Harry, who wants to kill him. Harry finds the hotel but is stopped at the door by the owner Maria, who has a baby with her.

Correct Answer: Superordinate Goal

Definition: an objective that requires multiple conflicting parties to cooperate in order to achieve it

Application: In order to avoid putting any innocent lives in danger, Ray calls for a temporary truce with Harry. In order to accomplish this goal, they must work together to stay peaceful while Maria and her baby could still get caught in the crossfire. However, Harry reminds us at the end that they are still planning on killing each other.
